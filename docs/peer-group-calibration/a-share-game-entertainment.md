# 1. A-Share Game Entertainment Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `互联网-互联网服务-游戏娱乐`.

The group mixes self-developed boutique studios, large mobile-game publishers, legacy IP operators, MMORPG/RPG developers, internet-cafe and social-entertainment platforms, cultural-media groups, acquired game studios, and ST turnaround cases. The review does not treat the game industry itself as a moat. The core question is whether a company has durable content, IP, R&D, publishing, long-life operation, or cross-business capability that cannot be quickly reproduced by simply spending more money.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when self-developed long-life products, disciplined publishing, globalized game operation, repeatable pipeline execution, and cash conversion are visible together.
2. Retain boundary cases when legacy IP, niche product longevity, or a company-level capability stack remains real, but scale, new-product proof, governance, or product-cycle execution is weaker than the direct-watch leaders.
3. Reject ordinary hit-driven studios, acquired game assets without platform control, weak IP-license stories, internet-cafe or social-entertainment services without proprietary content, mixed entertainment groups without segment leadership, and ST cases without exceptional independently verifiable capability.
4. A watched game company must be harder to beat than simply funding a new studio or buying traffic. Durable watch value requires evidence of product life, user retention, IP control, data-driven publishing, global operation, category reputation, or cross-business capability.
