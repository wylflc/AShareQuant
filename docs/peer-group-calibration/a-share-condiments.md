# 1. A-Share Condiments Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `食品饮料-食品-调味品`.

The group mixes national condiment brands, second-tier soy-sauce brands, zero-additive premium brands, hotpot and compound-seasoning brands, vinegar brands, mushroom-sauce and spice niches, B2B restaurant seasoning-solution companies, functional sugar and sugar-alcohol ingredient companies, MSG brands, flavor/additive suppliers, sugar processors, and misclassified transformation stories. The review separates category-defining brand/channel moats from ordinary packaged-food or commodity ingredients.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they have national category leadership, brand mindshare, dense channels, restaurant and household repeat purchase, scale, and multi-category extension.
2. Retain boundary cases when they have clear zero-additive/premium differentiation, second-tier but scaled brand assets, category-defining vinegar or hotpot-base brands, niche origin/recipe advantages, B2B restaurant-solution stickiness, or functional sugar/ingredient technology.
3. Reject legacy MSG brands, smaller duplicate compound-seasoning suppliers, ST condiment names, ordinary chicken-essence brands, flavor/additive suppliers without clear lock-in, commodity sugar processors, and misclassified daily-chemical transformation stories.
4. A watched condiment or food-ingredient company must show brand habit, channel control, restaurant workflow lock-in, functional ingredient technology, customer certification, or origin/recipe differentiation that a funded entrant cannot quickly copy.
