# 1. A-Share CXO And Pharmaceutical R&D Outsourcing Peer-Group Calibration

## 1.1 Purpose

This note starts A-share **Peer-Group Calibration** for CXO, CRDMO, CRO, CDMO, SMO, and pharmaceutical R&D service companies.

This group is separate from medical devices, innovative-drug developers, peptide/API manufacturers, and generic-drug manufacturers. The goal is to decide which listed companies deserve attention because their customer trust, regulatory quality systems, project continuity, chemistry or biology platforms, site networks, and global delivery capabilities are hard to replicate.

The key test is whether a well-funded entrant can defeat the company mainly by spending money. In CXO, capital can build labs, hire researchers, and acquire capacity, but cannot quickly buy:

1. long-cycle sponsor trust and repeated project flow;
2. regulatory audit history and GMP or GLP quality systems;
3. late-stage or commercial project continuity;
4. chemistry, biology, safety-evaluation, clinical-operation, or manufacturing know-how;
5. global customer coverage and cross-border compliance capability.

## 1.2 Evidence Boundary

This first pass uses company annual reports, company official result releases, exchange or CNINFO PDFs, professional research summaries, legal analysis of U.S. BIOSECURE risk, and local financial indicators for margins and cash flow.

Rows using research summaries or media coverage are acceptable for calibration discussion, but final deep review should replace them with direct annual-report PDFs, official investor-relations records, customer-project disclosures, quality-audit disclosures, and segment-margin tables wherever possible. Aggregator company introductions are not used as moat evidence.

## 1.3 Industry Context

CXO is structurally attractive when innovation funding is healthy, but it is not immune to cycles. Biotech financing cycles, U.S.-China policy risk, customer concentration, and price competition can turn a high-growth outsourcing platform into a low-return services business.

The calibrated rule should therefore separate:

1. **high-barrier CRDMO**: project continuity from discovery to commercial manufacturing;
2. **clinical CRO**: hospital/site network, MRCT experience, data management, and sponsor trust;
3. **specialized CDMO/API**: process technology, regulatory quality, and commercial project history;
4. **front-end molecule platforms**: high-SKU molecular building blocks, tool compounds, and citation/customer flywheels;
5. **labor-intensive services**: SMO or generic execution work that may be easier for larger platforms to replicate.

BIOSECURE and U.S. procurement risk must be treated as a real risk factor for Chinese CXO companies. It does not automatically destroy the moat, but it lowers the standard for relying on U.S. customer growth alone as proof of durability.

## 1.5 Reviewer Decision

The reviewer instruction for this round is: companies already marked as direct watch should be retained, while boundary companies should be decided by the existing standard rather than left as `needs_more_evidence`.

The accepted boundary standard is stricter than "has a real business." A boundary CXO company is retained only when evidence shows that a well-funded entrant or a larger retained platform could not easily copy the service and compress its economics. The practical tests are margin quality, product or service technical content, customer trust, project continuity, regulatory/quality-system history, network effects, and whether the company has a distinct role not already covered by stronger retained peers.

## 1.6 Company Comparisons

`药明康德` should be the A-share CXO core watch company. Its advantage is not merely large revenue. It has a global end-to-end CRDMO platform, strong D&M project funnel, late-stage and commercial projects, TIDES, testing, biology, chemistry, manufacturing, quality systems, and customer trust. The main counterargument is geopolitical risk, especially BIOSECURE implementation and U.S. customer exposure. That risk should cap confidence but does not by itself remove the company from the watchlist.

`康龙化成` should be retained as the second integrated platform candidate. It is smaller than 药明康德, but its lab service to CMC conversion, clinical CRO and SMO projects, commercial CDMO progress, and GLP-1 manufacturing agreement suggest that it is not simply a weak copy.

`凯莱英` should be retained as a high-margin small-molecule CDMO anchor. Its 2025 small-molecule CDMO margin evidence, commercial project count, phase III projects, and emerging business in ADC, peptides, oligonucleotides, and biologics support a differentiated CDMO thesis.

`泰格医药` is retained as a clinical CRO network leader. Its moat is not chemistry or manufacturing; it is clinical trial execution, hospital and site networks, MRCT experience, data/statistics, imaging, and sponsor trust. Margin pressure and扣非 profit decline are real warnings, but the business is not just low-end SMO labor.

`九洲药业` is retained with lower conviction as a specialized CDMO/API platform. It has acceptable margins, cash conversion, CDMO pipeline evidence, and emerging peptide or conjugation platforms. It is not stronger than 凯莱英, but it has enough independent depth for observation.

`皓元医药` is retained as a high-SKU molecular-building-block and tool-compound platform. Its front-end reagent business appears higher-margin and less like simple labor outsourcing. The back-end CDMO ramp still needs monitoring.

`药石科技` is rejected for now. It has molecular-building-block know-how and CDMO extension, but the shift toward development and commercial-stage business lowered gross margin quality. Current evidence does not justify retaining it beside 皓元医药 and larger CDMO platforms unless deeper review proves a materially stronger high-complexity building-block niche or customer lock-in.

`阳光诺和` is retained with lower conviction. Its unusually strong margins, high R&D ratio, orders, and integrated domestic drug R&D service model make it more than a generic labor-outsourcing case. The key monitoring item is whether its transition from generic-drug CRO to innovative-drug R&D services remains credible.

`昭衍新药` is rejected for now. GLP safety evaluation, animal facilities, pathology, and regulatory compliance are real barriers, but current laboratory-service profitability is weak and 2025 profit quality needs decomposition. It should not be retained only because the headline net profit rebounded.

`博腾股份` is rejected for now. Revenue recovered, but net margin and ROE remain weak, and current evidence does not show a clearly differentiated CDMO niche versus 凯莱英 or 九洲药业.

`美迪西` is rejected for now because weak margins and losses suggest current service capability is not protecting economics against broader preclinical CRO competition.

`睿智医药` is rejected for now because current profitability and margin evidence do not prove a hard-to-replicate niche.

`百诚医药` is rejected for now from the CXO group. It has high R&D intensity and possible innovative-pipeline optionality, but CRO revenue decline and losses mean the outsourcing moat is not yet proven. It may fit a later innovative-drug pipeline review better.

`普蕊斯` is rejected for now. SMO execution can have value, but the business is more labor and process intensive than high-technology CXO services and is already partially covered by 泰格医药's clinical ecosystem.

`ST诺泰` is reclassified. Its metrics are strong, but the company is better judged as a peptide API and drug manufacturer rather than as a general CXO peer.

## 1.7 Accepted Rules From This Group

1. **CRDMO Core Watch Requires Project Continuity**: retain companies that can move customers from discovery or early development into late-stage and commercial manufacturing.
2. **Quality Systems Are A Moat**: GMP, GLP, FDA or international audit history, quality systems, and regulatory execution should matter more than headline lab capacity.
3. **Clinical CRO Needs Network Proof**: hospital/site network, MRCT experience, data management, and sponsor trust can justify a clinical CRO watch company, but low-end SMO labor alone should not.
4. **Do Not Retain Turnaround Services Without Economics**: a CRO or CDMO company with weak margins and no clear differentiated platform should remain rejected even if industry demand is recovering.
5. **Front-End Molecule Platforms Can Qualify**: molecular building blocks, tool compounds, SKU breadth, customer citations, and synthesis difficulty can be a moat if margins and customer use support it.
6. **Geopolitical Risk Is A Core Constraint**: U.S. policy risk does not automatically reject a CXO leader, but it must reduce confidence and should be monitored as part of the thesis.
7. **Reject Weak Copies**: smaller CRO/CDMO companies should be rejected when they are mostly covered by 药明康德, 康龙化成, 凯莱英, 泰格医药, or 九洲药业 and lack an irreplaceable niche.
8. **Reclassify Mixed Drug Manufacturers**: peptide/API or innovative-drug companies should not be forced into the CXO group when their real thesis is product manufacturing or proprietary pipelines.
9. **Boundary Retention Requires Distinct Economics Or Capability**: a boundary company needs margin quality, customer trust, regulatory quality, project continuity, or product complexity that shows the service is not easily copied by capital.
10. **Do Not Retain Easy-Copy Services**: if a larger retained platform could reproduce the service mainly by hiring staff or adding capacity, and the smaller company lacks durable economics, reject it even if the industry itself is attractive.
