# 1. A-Share Metal Products Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `机械设备-金属制品-金属制品`.

The group is unusually broad. It mixes powder metallurgy, MIM and soft-magnetic materials, global hand tools, battery structural parts, aerospace superalloys, high-end cutting tools, special-alloy materials, high-end pressure vessels, prestressed rail materials, welding materials, magnet wire, wind-power metal parts, semiconductor-equipment precision parts, consumer hardware, metal packaging, wire rope, coated steel sheet, ordinary casting, and generic precision machining. The review separates true material/process/customer-lock-in moats from ordinary metal fabrication.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they have platform-level material/process capability, such as powder metallurgy and MIM, global tool brand/channel and supply-chain strength, power-battery structural-part customer lock-in, aerospace superalloys, or high-end CNC carbide inserts.
2. Retain boundary cases when they have narrower but visible qualification-heavy niches, including carbide tools, PCB micro-drills, high-end pressure vessels, special-material equipment, high-temperature alloys, advanced composites, prestressed rail materials, high-end welding materials, magnet wire, offshore wind fabrication, large wind castings, safety-critical wind fasteners, and semiconductor-equipment precision metal parts.
3. Reject ordinary metal packaging, consumer hardware, drinkware, kitchenware, household security hardware, coated sheet, wire rope, rigging, ordinary casting, precision stamping, precision machining, and weaker duplicate suppliers when their edge is mainly capacity, cost, customer service, or thematic end-market exposure.
4. A watched metal-products company must show that a well-funded entrant cannot quickly replicate its material recipe, process yield, customer qualification, brand/channel asset, safety-critical validation, or global supply-chain position.
