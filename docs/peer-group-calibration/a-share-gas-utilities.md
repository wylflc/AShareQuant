# 1. A-Share Gas Utilities Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `公用事业-燃气-燃气`.

Gas utilities have real local monopoly features, but the industry is regulated and usually lower growth. The review therefore avoids retaining every city-gas franchise. It prioritizes national gas procurement/distribution platforms, dense first-tier city-gas concessions, provincial pipeline roles, LNG supply-chain advantages, integrated clean-energy optionality, and distinct LNG/cryogenic gas-equipment technology.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they own national gas procurement/distribution platforms, dense first-tier city-gas concessions, or distinct LNG/cryogenic gas-equipment technology rather than ordinary local concession value alone.
2. Retain boundary cases when they have a larger regional city-gas franchise, provincial pipeline role, upstream/resource linkage, LNG supply-chain edge, or integrated clean-energy optionality not already fully covered by stronger retained peers.
3. Reject small local gas utilities, mixed utility/investment portfolios, commodity-exposed gas traders, ST cases, and regional concessions without scale, sourcing advantage, or integrated-energy differentiation.
4. Treat utility stability as a moat input, but cap the overall watchlist breadth because regulated returns and weak industry growth reduce long-term compounding potential.
