# 1. A-Share Semiconductor Materials Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `电子设备-半导体-半导体材料`.

The group includes silicon wafers, SiC substrates, CMP slurries and pads, specialty gases, precursors, sputtering targets, photomasks, wet electronic chemicals, quartz and ceramic components, independent testing, foundry/package-adjacent capability, laser chips, and some weak or misclassified consumer-electronics/materials companies. The review emphasizes fab qualification, purity/process control, yield learning, and customer validation rather than current short-term profit.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they own wafer, SiC, CMP, specialty-gas, precursor, target, critical process-chemical, ceramic/quartz, or equipment-platform capabilities with fab qualification and process know-how that capital alone cannot quickly replicate.
2. Retain boundary cases when they own a specific semiconductor-material, photomask, testing, foundry/package, laser-chip, ceramic/quartz, or wet-chemical niche not already fully covered by a stronger retained peer.
3. Reject consumer-electronics materials, IC-card/package commodity parts, diluted non-semiconductor businesses, weaker small silicon-wafer followers, and companies whose semiconductor exposure is not yet a distinct material or process moat.
4. Do not reject scarce semiconductor materials because of current losses or near-term cycle pressure; judge by material difficulty, customer validation, substitution scarcity, and normalized margin potential.
