# 1. A-Share Infrared Thermal Imaging And Special Optoelectronic Sensing Peer-Group Calibration

## 1.1 Purpose

This note carves the first workable subgroup out of the broad residual `电子设备-电子设备制造-电子设备制造` queue item.

The subgroup covers A-share listed companies whose main thesis is infrared thermal imaging, infrared detector chips, optoelectronic sensing, laser/optical systems, or special optoelectronic equipment. It is not a general consumer-electronics, EMS, laser-processing, display, connector, or semiconductor-design review.

## 1.2 Standard

Infrared thermal imaging has real barriers. A funded entrant can buy equipment and hire engineers, but cannot quickly reproduce detector chip process know-how, infrared focal-plane array manufacturing, chip-package-module integration, image processing algorithms, customer qualification, special-equipment project history, and batch delivery reliability.

The watchlist standard is still company-level. A company is retained only when it proves that its technology converts into durable platform economics, customer trust, or system-level irreplaceability. A company can be rejected even in a high-barrier industry if it is a weaker copy of the retained leaders.

## 1.4 Accepted Rules

1. **High-Barrier Industry Does Not Mean All Companies Qualify**: infrared sensing companies still need company-level proof of market position, customer trust, margin quality, and productized capability.
2. **Retain Differentiated Leaders**: keep multiple companies only when one has a detector/platform thesis and another has a separate system/equipment-integration thesis.
3. **Reject Weak Copies**: smaller infrared companies with real technology should still be rejected when their role is covered by stronger retained peers and current evidence does not show irreplaceable customer or product economics.
4. **Losses Are Not Automatic Rejection**: a loss-making company can re-enter if later evidence shows order recovery, customer lock-in, and technical capability converting into durable economics.

## 1.5 Remaining Work

The parent residual electronic-equipment group remains incomplete. Next subgroups should separate:

1. laser processing and additive manufacturing equipment;
2. optoelectronic sensing, machine vision, and industrial vision;
3. EMS and consumer-electronics manufacturing;
4. connectors, relays, precision components, and passive components;
5. display, PCB, and semiconductor-adjacent companies that should be reclassified into more precise groups.
