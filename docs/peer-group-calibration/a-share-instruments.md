# 1. A-Share Instruments Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `机械设备-通用设备-仪器仪表`.

The group mixes high-end electronic measurement, industrial process-control instruments, motion-control platforms, machine vision, precision metrology, scientific and analytical instruments, mass spectrometry, sensors, utility meters, environmental monitoring systems, battery testing, vehicle inspection, generic automation hardware, and small project-driven instrument niches. The review separates true measurement and control capability from low-barrier metering or integration businesses.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they own a broad and defensible platform in high-end electronic measurement, process-control instrumentation, machine vision, industrial sensors, or motion control.
2. Retain boundary cases when they own a narrower but real niche in precision metrology, analytical instruments, mass spectrometry, calibration, nondestructive testing, structural testing, battery testing, fiber-optic sensing, or utility metering with installed-base/customer-validation advantages.
3. Reject low-barrier or weaker-duplicate utility meters, project-heavy environmental monitoring systems, generic automation hardware, vehicle inspection equipment, small instrument niches without category control, and broad theme stories already covered by stronger retained peers.
4. Do not let current profit alone decide the result; a loss-making or ST company can remain watchable when the underlying instrument technology is scarce, but execution and governance risk can keep it boundary rather than direct watch.
