# 1. A-Share Lithium Resources And Lithium Salts Peer-Group Calibration

## 1.1 Purpose

This note starts A-share **Peer-Group Calibration** for lithium resources and lithium salts.

This group is separate from EV batteries, cathode materials, battery recycling, and broad strategic resources. The goal is to decide which listed companies deserve attention because they own or operate hard-to-replicate lithium resources, low-cost lithium extraction routes, or rare-light-metal resource platforms.

The key test is whether a well-funded entrant can defeat the company mainly by spending money. In lithium, capital can build conversion capacity quickly, and the industry has shown fast supply responses. That means a lithium company needs more than price beta, customer announcements, or nominal lithium-salt capacity. It should show scarce resource ownership, cost advantage, project execution, process capability, or a differentiated resource form that stronger peers do not already cover.

## 1.2 Evidence Boundary

This first pass uses company annual reports, half-year reports, quarterly reports, company sustainability or ESG reports, statutory disclosure mirrors, reputable annual-report coverage, IEA critical-mineral analysis, and USGS mineral statistics.

Rows using disclosure mirrors or media annual-report summaries are acceptable for calibration discussion, but final deep review should replace them with direct exchange or company annual-report PDFs wherever possible. Aggregator company introductions are not used as moat evidence.

## 1.3 Industry Context

Lithium deserves attention as a strategic resource, but it is not identical to copper or rare earths. IEA's 2025 outlook says battery-metal supply expanded quickly and put downward pressure on prices, while also projecting that lithium demand can grow strongly and that announced mine supply still leaves a large implied 2035 deficit under stated policies. USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026 is the earliest comprehensive global source for 2025 mineral production data.

This creates a two-sided rule:

1. lithium companies should not be rejected just because current profit is weak in a downcycle;
2. lithium companies should not be retained just because lithium prices may rise.

The watchlist standard should emphasize reserve quality, cost curve, production ramp, resource self-sufficiency, processing know-how, and whether the company's advantage is already covered by a stronger retained peer.

## 1.5 Preliminary Comparison

`盐湖股份` remains a watch company from the strategic-resource group. It is not a hard-rock lithium peer, but its 察尔汗盐湖钾锂 resource position, high margins, and domestic resource constraint justify keeping it as a salt-lake anchor.

`天齐锂业` should be retained because Greenbushes exposure gives it one of the clearest world-class hard-rock lithium resource positions among A-share companies. The current score should not be anchored to one year's profit recovery or weakness.

`赣锋锂业` should be retained separately from 天齐. Its advantage is not a single best mine, but a global multi-resource portfolio, lithium-chemical process ecosystem, metal lithium, and recycling optionality. The risk is that integration becomes too broad and capital intensive, so it should be monitored for debt and project execution.

`中矿资源` should be retained because it is not only a lithium company. Bikita, Tanco, cesium, rubidium, and geological exploration capability give it a differentiated scarce-resource angle that is not covered by 天齐 or 赣锋.

`川能动力`, `藏格矿业`, and `融捷股份` are boundary cases. They each have real resource evidence, but they are not clearly in the same class as the retained global or national anchors. The reviewer should decide whether the A-share watchlist should reserve attention slots for second-tier domestic hard-rock or salt-lake lithium platforms.

`盛新锂能` and `雅化集团` should not be retained merely because they have lithium output or overseas lithium-resource exposure. Their resource positions are not yet strong enough versus 赣锋, 天齐, 中矿资源, and 盐湖股份.

`永兴材料` and `江特电机` are lithium-mica examples. Lithium mica can be strategically relevant for domestic supply, but the watchlist bar should be higher because cost competitiveness and environmental processing complexity are harder to defend versus high-quality hard-rock and salt-lake assets.

`西藏矿业` has resource scarcity through 扎布耶, but project execution and stable low-cost output remain insufficiently proven for watchlist inclusion. This is a reject-for-now decision, not an insufficient-evidence decision.

`科达制造` should be reclassified. Its Blue Lithium exposure is through a material equity stake, while its own main business is machinery, overseas building materials, and lithium-battery materials. If the thesis is salt-lake lithium, `盐湖股份` is the cleaner listed-company representative.

## 1.6 Draft Rules To Test With Reviewer

1. **Lithium Resource Watch Requires Resource Plus Execution**: retain companies that combine scarce lithium resource access with cost, production, processing, or project-execution proof.
2. **Lithium-Salt Capacity Alone Is Not A Moat**: conversion capacity can be built with capital. Retain processing companies only when resource self-supply, customer validation, and process economics are source-backed.
3. **Retain Differentiated Resource Forms**: hard-rock lithium, salt-lake lithium, and rare-light-metal platforms can all be retained if each has a distinct hard-to-replicate advantage.
4. **Use The Dominance Rejection Test Inside Lithium**: reject companies whose lithium thesis is comprehensively covered by 天齐, 赣锋, 盐湖股份, or 中矿资源 unless they have an independent niche.
5. **Lithium Mica Needs A Higher Bar**: lithium-mica companies need stronger cost, resource-grade, environmental, and process evidence before watchlist inclusion.
6. **Do Not Reject Solely For Current Losses**: a loss-making lithium company can remain watchable if the loss comes from cycle timing while the resource and cost advantage are strong.
7. **Do Reject Weak Cost Positions**: if losses or low margins reflect weak cost curve and no hard-to-replicate resource or process moat, rejection is appropriate.
8. **Non-Controlled Resource Exposure Is Not Enough**: a company with only a financial stake in a lithium-resource asset should usually be reclassified unless it controls the operating resource thesis.

## 1.7 Analyst Dissent Policy

1. I would dissent if `天齐锂业` or `赣锋锂业` are rejected solely because lithium is cyclical or because one year's profit is weak.
2. I would dissent if `中矿资源` is rejected without addressing its cesium, rubidium, Bikita, Tanco, and exploration advantages.
3. I would accept rejecting `川能动力`, `藏格矿业`, or `融捷股份` if the reviewer wants a concentrated list and believes second-tier domestic lithium resources are covered by stronger retained peers.
4. I would not dissent if `盛新锂能`, `雅化集团`, `永兴材料`, `江特电机`, `西藏矿业`, or `科达制造` remain excluded under current evidence.
5. I would dissent if `江特电机` or `西藏矿业` are rejected only because they are currently loss-making. The defensible rejection reason is lack of proven low-cost and project-execution advantage versus stronger retained peers.
