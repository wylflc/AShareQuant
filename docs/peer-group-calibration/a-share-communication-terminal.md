# 1. A-Share Communication Terminal Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `信息技术-通信设备-通信终端设备`.

The peer group mixes global enterprise communication endpoints, professional mobile radio, enterprise networking, smart-grid communication chips, secure smart-card/eSIM platforms, RF antennas and shielding materials, optical modules, Beidou/GNSS, defense communications, CPE/STB vendors, handset or IoT terminal ODMs, and project-based communication integrators. The review therefore separates product-platform and qualification moats from low-margin manufacturing scale.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they own global niche brand economics, protected professional/dedicated-network communications, enterprise-network product platforms, Beidou/GNSS navigation capabilities, or defense/special-industry communication qualifications that capital cannot quickly replicate.
2. Retain boundary cases only when they have a differentiated secure-card/eSIM, RF/antenna/materials, optical-module, grid-communication, IoT-terminal, railway/defense dispatch, or satellite-communication niche not already fully covered by a stronger retained peer.
3. Reject low-margin ODM/JDM terminal manufacturers, ordinary CPE/STB or handset terminal vendors, mature smart-card companies without clear platform control, and project-based system integrators when a better-funded entrant or stronger peer can compete without hard technology barriers.
4. Current profitability is not the main determinant, but low normalized margins help identify ODM or commodity hardware businesses where the company lacks pricing power.
