# 1. A-Share Medical Device Peer-Group Calibration

## 1.1 Purpose

This note starts A-share **Peer-Group Calibration** for high-end medical devices and medical-device platforms.

Medical devices are not one homogeneous industry. A company can be worth watching when it has a hard-to-replace position in a distinct clinical or equipment niche, even if another retained company is stronger in a different niche. The correct test is **Differentiated Peer Retention**: keep multiple peers only when their advantages are meaningfully different and durable.

## 1.2 Evidence Boundary

This first pass uses company annual reports or annual-report mirrors, annual-report summaries from designated financial media, company filings, NMPA registration-policy information, and reputable securities research summaries.

Rows marked with weaker source quality should receive direct exchange or company annual-report PDFs before final rule freezing. Aggregator company profiles are not used as moat evidence.

## 1.3 Industry Context

Medical devices have structural demand from aging, hospital capacity upgrades, domestic substitution, and global expansion. The 2025 NMPA registration report shows continued innovation-device review activity, which supports the idea that regulatory approval, clinical validation, and product quality systems are real barriers.

The industry is also exposed to procurement cycles, anti-corruption campaigns, volume-based procurement, hospital budget constraints, and global regulatory approval risk. A high score should therefore require more than "国产替代"; it should show hospital adoption, product breadth, installed base, clinical trust, and evidence that a stronger retained peer does not already cover the same advantage.

## 1.5 Analyst Dissent Policy

1. If `迈瑞医疗` is rejected mainly because 2025 earnings declined, I would strongly dissent. Short-term procurement and policy pressure should not erase a platform-level medical-device moat.
2. If `联影医疗` is rejected because it is smaller than Mindray, I would strongly dissent. High-end imaging is a distinct and hard-to-replace niche.
3. If `惠泰医疗` is rejected only because it is smaller, I would dissent. The electrophysiology and vascular-intervention niche may have clinical adoption and consumable lock-in that a general platform does not cover.
4. I would only softly dissent on `心脉医疗`, `南微医学`, `开立医疗`, and `新产业`; each needs a sharper judgment on whether its specialty is truly irreplaceable or already covered by a stronger retained peer.
5. I would not dissent if `鱼跃医疗` is rejected from this group, because it belongs in a home-healthcare peer group. I would not dissent if `乐普医疗` or `新华医疗` are rejected under the current evidence.

## 1.6 Proposed Rule Hypotheses

These are hypotheses for reviewer challenge, not final rules.

1. **Core Watch**: retain companies with a broad medical-device platform or high-end equipment platform, global/tertiary-hospital validation, strong installed base, service network, and continuous product iteration.
2. **Specialized Watch**: retain smaller companies only when their clinical niche is meaningfully different from retained leaders and has hard-to-replace doctor adoption, regulatory approvals, recurring consumables, or treatment-pathway lock-in.
3. **Dominance Rejection**: reject a company when its main product line is already covered by a stronger retained leader and the company lacks a distinct niche.
4. **Reclassification**: move companies into a more suitable group when their real advantage is outside high-end hospital devices, such as home healthcare, consumer medical devices, or medical aesthetics.

## 1.7 Reviewer Questions

1. Do you agree that `迈瑞医疗` and `联影医疗` should be core watch companies?
2. Should `惠泰医疗` be retained as a specialized high-value device leader?
3. Should `心脉医疗` and `南微医学` be retained as specialized watch candidates, or rejected because they are too narrow?
4. Should `开立医疗` and `新产业` be rejected under dominance by stronger platforms, or retained because endoscopy and chemiluminescence are sufficiently distinct?
5. Do you agree that `鱼跃医疗` should be moved to a home-healthcare peer group and that `乐普医疗`, `新华医疗` should be rejected for now?
