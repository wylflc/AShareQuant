# 1. A-Share Renewable-Power Peer-Group Review

## 1.1 Purpose

This note records the company-by-company review for `公用事业-电力-新能源发电`.

The group mixes nuclear power, wind and solar flagship platforms, large central-SOE renewable platforms, provincial renewable operators, private project developers, integrated-energy service companies, EPC and solution providers, and companies whose renewable-power tag is incidental to a different business. The review separates scarce regulated assets and resource-development platforms from ordinary power-plant capacity.

## 1.2 Retention Standard

1. Retain direct-watch companies when they have scarce nuclear licensing and operating records, global or national renewable scale, central-SOE platform status, low-cost financing, project acquisition advantages, offshore wind or mega-base experience, and durable operating systems.
2. Retain boundary cases when they have visible parent support, differentiated project mix, provincial resource control, specialist wind-operation history, or stable hydro/renewable assets, but are still weaker than national leaders.
3. Reject ordinary renewable developers, regional operators, integrated-energy stories, EPC and solution providers, investment-holding profiles, and mixed businesses when the moat is mainly project finance, ordinary asset development, or end-market growth.
4. Stable power cash flow is not enough. A watched company must show that a well-funded entrant could not quickly replicate its resource access, license position, grid connection, dispatch/consumption advantage, financing cost, project references, or operational reliability.
